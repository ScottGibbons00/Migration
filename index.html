The movement of a person or people from one country, locality, place of residence, etc., to settle in another

Although forced migration because of climate change has yet to peak, its effects are felt in many ecosystems. As we continue to slip into apocolypse, climate change will move humans. In many cases, minorities will be foreced to move first. Without change, millions, perhaps billions, of people will have no choice to move. "Migration" explores the setting of these examples of movement, and shows the systems that support environmental injustice.

Stories from the Almora district in the Indian Himalayas.

  • "It used to be that, when monsoons came and clouds thundered a lot, there were so many trees that the rain would be soaked up by the trees themselves. Now the monsoons create destruction everywhere."

  • "Migration is the only option...more than 50 per cent of the people in our village have migrated...Either we migrate, or we drag ourselves to death."

  • "if it rains too much, if the land is weak, then everything breaks down"

Rising temperatures have turned more snow into rain in the Himalayas. Worsening monsoons have washed away landscapes, making agriculture an un-winnable battle. Floods and erosion destroyed homes. Without food or shelter, these villagers are forced to migrate elsewhere.

Within the century, one billion people will be living on a coastline.

Rising sea levels force communities to build levees, dams, or raise buidlings. Eventually, however, people will have no choice but to move inland. Hundreds of years of racism have resulted in unequal geographies, where minorities are in more danger than white people. Coastal counties have proprotionally more African Americans. These geological differences will force more inequalities as these African Americans are forced to migrate.

Deforestation forces migration in multiple ways

  • Examples of slow violence, such as deforestion, have shown to drive long-term migration. Deforestation destroys ecosystems and biodiversity, which effects nearby communities.

  • In certain regions, deforestation disrupts the water cycle, removes the mesh that hold soil in place, and eliminates nutrients. This causes desertification, and prevents people from living or farming on previously healthy land.

  • Forests are often cleared for livestock to provide to global demand for meat. Intense farming and raising of livestock removes vegetation and nutrients from the land, thus making it unlivable.

The toxic chemicals released by factories force neighbors to choose to leave or face cancer or death

Take the example of The Marathon Petroleum Corporation's Oil Refinery in Boynton, Michigan

This oil refinery is situated in a town that is 75% African American, and causes thousands to become sick. Nearby in Detroit, there are twice as many deaths from air pollution as gun violence, and the "impacts of poor air quality disproportionately fall on poor and minority populations." Many left if they could afford to leave. When the refinery expanded, Marathon bought the houses of a white community nearby to mitigate the effects of their pollution on the community. Meanwhile, the predominantly black communtiy of Boynton was not offered any buyout opportunities. While some were given the opporunity to migrate with financial support, minorities were either forced to migrate or suffer through health conditions.
